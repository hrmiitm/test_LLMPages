When the lacquer cooled, Ashravan opened his eyes to a world that recognized him before he recognized it. The palace had learned to live without him; the river that had once been his childhood boundary had been asked to forget. Shai had put him back together as carefully as a master artisan might repair a clock—aligning gears, resetting springs, smoothing the broken enamel of a life. He felt the repairs like a map under his ribs; memories stitched to purpose, intent threaded through bone.

Walking the corridors, he heard talk of succession as though a man could be swapped like a seal. He saw old friends who now measured him in hesitation and men who hoped his return would be a quiet thing. In the antechamber, Shai’s small mark remained hidden in the grain of a vase—two incised lines like a pair of keys. He touched them and felt gratitude and a pressure that would not relent: there were things fixed in him beyond flesh.

When the conspirators moved, they brought with them a witness stamp and a brazier, rituals meant to prove continuity by oath. They believed the restored were hollow, and a stamp could expose the hollow. Ashravan walked into their circle carrying a lacquered panel Shai had given back to him, its inlay faint with a secret varnish. The room smelled of smoke and certainty.

They demanded proof. He offered honesty: not a rote repetition of decrees but the hidden ledger Shai had helped him find—names and promises, the soft rot of an Empire traded in chambers and bottles. He let the brazier take the witness stamp. The metal shrieked once, then softened. Heat burned his palm; he let the stamp fall.

"A ruler must remember why we change," he said, voice steady as a bell under wind. "It is not sameness that secures us, but the courage to break what is rotten."

Silence snapped like a bowstring. The conspirators’ certainty faltered; the Arbiter’s eye widened. In that heat, Ashravan chose not merely to be returned, but to act—fusing the memory of what he had been with a resolve that would either save the Empire or set it afire. The brazier’s embers rent the night as the chamber decided: to preserve sameness, or to answer for the future. Ashravan stepped toward the light and, for the first time since being remade, felt the full weight of his choice.
